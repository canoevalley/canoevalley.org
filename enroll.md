<h1>Enrollment at Canoe Valley School</h1>

<p> We are welcoming enrollment interest from families of students aged 12-18
for our anticipated fall 2027* opening. We are planning on a first-year
enrollment size of about 15 students, and aim to grow within a few years to
about 30-40 students in two mixed-age programs, a lower and an upper level. Our
enrollment application will be available this coming fall/winter; we anticipate
significant interest, so please consider joining our pre-enrollment waitlist
[LINK]. If we receive more applications than we have spots available, we will
prioritize applications in order of pre-enrollment waitlist sign-up date.  </p>

<p> In line with our core institutional value of accessibility (see
Institutional Characteristics below), we aim to make enrolling at Canoe Valley
School as affordable and flexible as we can. Upon accreditation, we will be
able to offer families access to the State of Iowa's Educational Savings
Account (ESA) program to offset tuition. To ensure the financial stability and
institutional thriving of the school, and in light of the financial picture
reported by families in the feasibility study included in our Long Range Plan
[LINK], tuition will also include an annual supplement paid by families in
addition to ESA per-pupil funding, set at an accessible level. We are currently
exploring scholarship as well as sliding-scale options for the tuition
supplement for families in financial need.  </p>

<p> The school is also exploring at least one flexible schedule enrollment
option: a half-time schedule that would allow students to pursue part of their
education at home or at another institution, part at Canoe Valley, and if a
high school diploma is desired, to graduate from either Canoe Valley or another
institution. Note that this option would not include access to the ESA program,
which requires at least 75% full-time enrollment. Note also that the
advancement pathway we are exploring for half-time students will depend on
accreditor approval.  </p>
