# About Canoe Valley School

<figure>
<figcaption>
Our Mission:
</figcaption>
<blockquote>Canoe Valley School aims to support students as they cultivate
their whole selves, and their connection to place, in order that they might
find their path in life and proceed boldly, with creativity, care and
competency. Students, teachers, parents, and community members pursue this work
together through self-directed, relational learning, and as a whole the school
community aims to serve the place we live, and to create a more beautiful,
just, and abundant world.
</blockquote>
</figure>

We are an emerging secondary school in rural Winneshiek County, located on
a 40-acre campus tucked away in a lush tributary valley of Canoe Creek. Our
vision for education is both old and new, drawing on longstanding alternative
educational traditions like Waldorf and Montessori, but also arising from the
educational history of this place and from the people who have come together to
found the school. Above all we aim to help students grow as whole persons,
supporting their self-motivation, self-understanding, healthy relationships,
and sense of responsibility and purpose, in addition to their knowledge
foundations. We pay particular attention to students' connection to place,
believing that strengthening our relationship to the natural world and to
community leads to the deepest kind of learning, and to a more just and
abundant world.



Canoe Valley's thoughtfully designed learning program pursues these goals using
an approach we call self-directed learning, together, in which student
curiosity and agency drives a project-based curriculum, and teachers spark,
inspire and deepen learning. Peers, parents, community members, and the natural
world are close companions in the learning process. Our program offers abundant
opportunities for exploration and play outdoors, shared outdoor work, as well
as occasions for deep attention, mindfulness/contemplative reflection, and
healthy relationship building. 



We provide a high-quality foundation in core skills and knowledge, as well as
in-depth and customizable preparation for college and other post-secondary
pathways. We take an interdisciplinary, whole-person and place-based approach
to academic subject areas, and offer  supplemental programs in reading and
math. Co-curricular offerings include a mentorship program, apprenticeship
opportunities, student participation in school committees and governance, and
much more.
